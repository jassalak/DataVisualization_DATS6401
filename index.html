And Analysis



  • Tree map that plots the average sale price by State. The darker the green, the higher the price.

  • DC has the highest sale price (on average)

  • VA and MD are second and third, respecitively



  • The State Dashboard utilizes several lollipop and bar graphs to display four metrics by State. Hover over the Filter to focus in on one State.

  • a. DC and VA have higher than average sale price (average: $351k).

  • b. The gold-diamonds depict average homes sold. NC currently has the highest.

  • c. PA has the highest average days on market.

  • d. WV and NC have the lowest and highest average number of new listings by State, respectively.



  • The City Dashboard has the same layout as the State Dashboard but can Filter on State AND City.





  • The GeoSpacial Bubble chart shows the median sale price (size) by City by State (color).

  • The dataset has 6 States, yet only in certain geographical areas. For this reason, average was used for all the analysis and results.



  • The Splicer graph is the most versatile display.

  • The x-axis is Time, and can be set to varying date granularities (Month, Quarter, Year)

  • The y-axis dynamically displays one out of five metrics.

  • Other filters include State and City filters.

Condegram Spiral Plot





  • The Spiral Plot is a Horizontal Bar Graph wrapped around itself.

  • This type of graph takes up less real-estate.

  • The x-axis is by Date, then by State (color).

  • The y-axis is the average Median Sale Price.

  • By color, the bars are increasing. This shows there is a steady rise in sale price by State over time.

    Conclusions

    Results

    • DC and NC are the ideal States if you are a seller and buyer, respectively.

    • DC has the highest sale price, and lowest days on market. They are also middle of the pack when it comes to new listings, suggesting new potential buyers will have options but not too many options.

    • NC has lower than average sale prices and sell the highest homes per average.

    • Houses in NC stay on the market almost three months, suggesting the buyer does not have to rush.
